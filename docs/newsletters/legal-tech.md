## 4. Operational Notes for AgentMail Intake

- **Plain HTML preferred.** All Substack and Beehiiv newsletters deliver clean HTML — safe for the Haiku classifier. Avoid Substack "Notes"-only senders.
- **Forwarding risk.** None of the above are known to block forwarding. Two soft flags: (a) LinkedIn newsletters (e.g., *Notes to My Legal Self*) deliver from `linkedin.com` and may rewrite links; (b) Law360 emails contain heavily personalized tracking links — test before relying.
- **Paid newsletters to flag in pipeline:** Law360 / Law360 Pulse, Cybersecurity Law Report, some ALM titles (American Lawyer, NYLJ premium), Practical Law Connect. Free headlines still useful via web-scrape fallback.
- **Domain disambiguation for the classifier:** several publications share the `law.com` domain (ALM properties). Suggest classifying by the `List-ID` or first-line "from" subdomain (e.g., `legaltechnews@law.com` vs `litigationdaily@law.com`) rather than just domain.
- **Substack sender pattern:** all Substack newsletters originate from `substack.com` (often `noreply@substack.com` or `<author-handle>@substack.com`). Use `List-ID` header to disambiguate Original Jurisdiction vs Jordan Furlong vs Hyperdimensional.
- **Beehiiv sender pattern:** typically `<newsletter-name>@beehiiv.com` or a custom domain — Brainyacts uses `thebrainyacts.beehiiv.com`.
