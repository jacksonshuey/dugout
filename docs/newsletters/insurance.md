## Notes on Constraints

- **All recommended newsletters are plain HTML email** as far as we can verify (Wells Media, ALM, Arizent, Substack, Omeda all use standard HTML templates that AgentMail will parse cleanly).
- **Forwarding:** Substack and the Wells Media properties (IJ, CM, Claims Journal) all permit forwarding. Insurance Day (Lloyd's List Intelligence) ToS may restrict redistribution — flag if Dugout's webhook is treated as redistribution; safer to skip until legal-reviewed.
- **Paid flags:** Business Insurance (magazine paid, email free); Insurance Day (fully paid); Captive Review (freemium); S&P Global (data products paid, newsletter free).
- **Sender-domain caveat:** Domains marked as Omeda (`wellsmedia.omeda.com`, `omeda.com`) are ESP-routed — the Haiku 4.5 classifier should key off List-Unsubscribe / From-display-name, not just the bare domain.

## Surprises / Sender-domain gaps to flag to the Dugout team

1. **Wells Media uses one shared Omeda sending domain** for IJ + Carrier Management + Claims Journal — three newsletters, one sender pattern. Classifier needs to disambiguate on subject line or List-ID header, not From: alone.
2. **Coverager is self-hosted** (not Substack despite the format) — if AgentMail has any deliverability quirks with smaller senders, Coverager is the one to monitor.
3. **No good free Lloyd's/London-market daily** that allows redistribution. Insurance Day is the right source but paid + likely ToS-restricted. Intelligent Insurer + Reinsurance News are the best free substitute.
4. **No US state-DOI-specific newsletter worth subscribing to** at the AE level — NAIC CIPR publishes irregularly and the state DOI bulletins are too granular. Recommend Dugout poll the NAIC newsroom RSS directly rather than via email.
5. **The "Future of Insurance" by Bryan Falchuk is a podcast, not a newsletter** — frequently mis-cited as a newsletter. Dropped from list. Sub to the podcast separately if AEs want it.
