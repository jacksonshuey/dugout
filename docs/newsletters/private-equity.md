## Notes / Caveats

- **PEI Group dominance.** PEI publishes 8+ of the trade publications listed (PEI, PE Hub, Buyouts, PDI, Secondaries Investor, Infra Investor, Private Funds CFO, New Private Markets). They share one sender domain root and one subscription team. Consider whether Dugout subscribes to one paid account and routes via shared inbox.
- **Paid-newsletter strategy.** WSJ Pro PE, Bloomberg Deals, PEI's trade pubs, and Dry Powder (Puck) are the four highest-signal paid options. Of those, **WSJ Pro Private Equity** is probably the highest ROI per dollar for a Checkbox AE — it's daily, deep, and the LP/GP coverage maps directly to Checkbox's buyer universe.
- **Forwarding/HTML reliability.** Substack newsletters (#11, #12, #13, #15) are plain-HTML and forward cleanly. Bloomberg and FT newsletters have heavier tracking pixels but the text content survives forwarding. NYT DealBook uses image-heavy templates — text extraction may need extra cleanup at the classifier stage.
- **Avoid.** Did not include Goldman Sachs / Lazard "research" emails — those are paywalled to institutional clients and won't ingest reliably. Did not include LinkedIn newsletters — they don't deliver via SMTP and would require LinkedIn scraping instead.
- **Coverage gap.** No strong free newsletter exists for **sovereign-wealth-fund flow** specifically (GIC, ADIA, Mubadala, PIF). The closest proxy is II Daily + P&I + Alt Goes Mainstream. Worth flagging as a future build: a Dugout-internal SWF tracker.
